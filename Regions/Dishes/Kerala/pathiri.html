Pathiri

Pathiri is a soft flatbread made from rice flour, prominent in the Malabar cuisine of Kerela and is commonly eaten within the Muslim community.

The basic dough is made from rice flour, salt, and water, but many additional ingredients can be added to make diverse variations of pathiri. Typically served with a rich, thick curry dish, usually meat such as chicken or beef, pathiri can also be eaten as a sweet dish with coconut milk and jaggery. Pathiri can be found served at special occasions, celebrations, and festivals, making it a distinct and culturally significant dish.
