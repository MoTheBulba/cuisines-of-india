Meghalaya

Meghalaya is located in northeastern India, and possess some of the most biodiverse landscapes in the world, and is especially known for its dramatic scenery often shrouded in clouds.

The state's culinary heritage reflects the rich cultural tapestry of its indigenous communities: the Khasis, Jaintia, and Garos. While rice and pork are common across these communities, each tribe has its unique culinary traditions and preferred ingredients. For instance, fermented soybean features prominently in Khasi and Jaintia cuisines, whereas the Garos have a penchant for kalchi, a type of cooking soda.
