Dalma

Dalma is a comforting lentil curry from Odisha, typically prepared with raw papaya and other assorted vegetables.

This dish has numerous variations, with each household having its own unique recipe. The tempering, or tadka, of spices and aromatics are fried in ghee for a rich and deep flavour. While raw papaya is traditionally preferred, bring a sweet and sour taste, ripe papaya can be used for a sweeter flavour profile.

Dalma holds significant cultural importance in Odisha and is associated with Lord Jagannath, a revered deity in the region. It is commonly served in temples as well as in households, often garnished with grated coconut and fresh coriander. It pairs well with rice or flatbread.
