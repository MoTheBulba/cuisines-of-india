Manipur

Manipur is a northeastern state of India, and is famous for its breathtaking natural landscapes and a rich wartime history, commemorated through numerous memorials.

The cuisine of Manipur includes mild flavours and uses local nutritious ingredients with minimal additional oil, rendering it a notably healthy cuisine. Steaming is a prevalent cooking method, often consisting of various local herbs that produce a wonderful aroma. A distinctive element of Manipuri cuisine is the use of ngari, a fermented fish ingredient, that gives a unique umami flavour. Meals in Manipur typically include rice accompanied by various of fish or vegetable dishes, reflecting the region's diverse landscape and culture.
