Farata

Farata is a type of flatbread made from maida (plain flour), offering a soft and fluffy texture distinct from the typical atta (whole wheat) flatbreads found across India.

It is excellent for soaking up thick curries and gravies. Often served alongside seafood dishes, particularly fish curry, farata is usually eaten for breakfast but can be served at other mealtimes too.
