Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep is an Indian union territory consisting of 36 islands in the Arabian Sea.

It is a famed holiday destination for its luxurious beaches and relaxing atmosphere. Famed for its abundant marine life, Lakshadweep's cuisine revolves prominently around fresh seafood. Among the 10 habitable islands of Lakshadweep, Minicoy stands out as the main holiday destination. Influenced by the Maldives, Sri Lanka, and Myanmar, Minicoy's cuisine presents a delicious fusion of flavours and ingredients that are enjoyed by both tourists and locals alike.
