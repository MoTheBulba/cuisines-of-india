Puducherry

Puducherry, a charming union territory in South India and boarded by the state of Tamil Nadu, bears the rich legacy of its French colonial past.

Renowned as a favoured holiday destination, both domestically and internationally, Puducherry captivates visitors with its well-preserved colonial architecture, picturesque seafronts, and beautiful beaches. The cuisine of Puducherry reflects its unique cultural amalgamation, blending influences from both French and Tamil culinary traditions, often referred to as Franco-Tamil cuisine.

This distinctive cuisine is not only delicious but also reflects the region's diverse colonial history. Croissants and baguettes are popular alongside the traditional Tamil fare, which includes a sub-cuisine known as Creole cuisine further adding to the diverse foods found in Puducherry. Additionally, many popular Puducherry dishes have Vietnamese and Malay influences, highlighting the territory as a vibrant melting pot of flavours and cultures.
